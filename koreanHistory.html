KINGS OF JOSEON · 조선의 왕들

The Joseon Dynasty lasted over 500 years (1392–1910) with 27 kings. Here are the most important ones — kings who changed Korea forever. Click any card to read their story and take a quiz!

Featured Kings · 주요 왕들 — Click to explore
1대 · 1392–1398
태조 이성계
King Taejo · Founder of Joseon
A great military general who overthrew the Goryeo dynasty and founded Joseon. He moved the capital to Hanyang — today's Seoul!
🏰 Dynasty Founder→
4대 · 1418–1450
세종대왕
King Sejong the Great
Korea's most beloved king. He invented Hangeul (한글) — the Korean alphabet — so ordinary people could read and write. A genius in science, music, and culture.
📚 Scholar King→
10대 · 1494–1506
연산군
Yeonsangun · The Tyrant King
Joseon's most infamous tyrant. He killed scholars, forced people to become his entertainers, and was eventually overthrown. One of the most dramatic stories in Korean history.
😈 Tyrant King→
14대 · 1567–1608
선조
King Seonjo · The Imjin War King
King during Japan's massive invasion (임진왜란). He famously fled the capital leaving citizens behind — but Admiral Yi Sun-sin saved the country.
⚔️ Imjin War Era→
21대 · 1724–1776
영조
King Yeongjo · The Reform King
Reigned 52 years — the longest in Joseon history. A great reformer, but remembered for one of history's most tragic decisions: executing his own son by locking him in a rice chest.
📖 Longest Reign→
26대 · 1863–1907
고종
King Gojong · First Emperor
Korea's last powerful ruler and first Emperor of the Korean Empire. He tried to modernize Korea but Japan was too powerful. In 1910, Japan annexed Korea, ending 518 years of Joseon.
👑 Last Emperor→

KOREAN HEROES · 한국의 영웅들

These people were not kings — but they changed Korean history with their courage, brilliance, and sacrifice. Click any card to discover their incredible stories.

Heroes · 영웅들 — Click to explore
1545–1598 · 임진왜란
⚓ 이순신 장군
Admiral Yi Sun-sin · Korea's Greatest Hero
Never lost a single battle. With only 13 ships he defeated a Japanese fleet many times larger. Greatly improved the Turtle Ship (거북선). Korea's most celebrated hero of all time.
🐢 Never Lost a Battle→
1879–1910 · 독립운동
안중근
Ahn Jung-geun · Independence Martyr
A Korean independence activist who assassinated Japan's top leader to resist colonization. Executed at 31, he became one of Korea's greatest national heroes.
🕊️ Independence Hero→
1902–1920 · 삼일운동
유관순
Yu Gwan-sun · The Joan of Arc of Korea
A 16-year-old student who led massive independence protests against Japan. Arrested, tortured, and died in prison at 18 — but never gave up. One of Korea's most beloved heroes.
💪 Student Hero→

임진왜란
The Japanese Invasion · Imjin War
Japan invaded Korea with 150,000 soldiers. Nearly every city was burned. But Admiral Yi Sun-sin and his turtle ships (거북선) fought back and saved Korea.

Japan ruled Korea for 35 years. Koreans were forced to use Japanese names and language. This is the main reason Korea and Japan still have tension today.

역사
KOREAN HISTORY TIMELINE · 한국 역사 타임라인

5,000 years of Korean history — from ancient kingdoms to modern K-pop. Here's how it all connects.

Timeline · 시간순서
2333 BC

고조선 · Gojoseon — The First Kingdom

According to legend, Dangun (단군) founded the first Korean kingdom. This is why Koreans say their history is 5,000 years old. October 3rd is still celebrated as National Foundation Day (개천절).

57 BC–668 AD

삼국시대 · Three Kingdoms Era

Three kingdoms — Goguryeo (고구려), Baekje (백제), and Silla (신라) — competed for 700 years. Eventually Silla unified Korea. This era inspired countless Korean dramas and movies!

918–1392

고려 · Goryeo Dynasty — Where "Korea" Comes From

The word "Korea" comes from "Goryeo." Famous for beautiful celadon pottery (청자) and the world's first metal movable type — invented 200 years before Gutenberg in Europe!

1392–1910

조선 · Joseon Dynasty — 518 Years

Korea's longest dynasty. King Sejong invented Hangeul (한글). Japan invaded during the Imjin War (1592). Eventually Japan colonized Korea in 1910, ending Joseon after 518 years.

1910–1945

일제강점기 · Japanese Colonial Period

Japan ruled Korea for 35 painful years. Koreans were forced to use Japanese names and speak Japanese at school. Korea was liberated in 1945 when Japan lost WWII.

1950–1953

한국전쟁 · The Korean War

North Korea invaded the South. The U.S. and 15 other UN nations helped the South; China helped the North. A ceasefire was signed in 1953 — but no peace treaty. The war technically never ended.

1988

서울 올림픽 · Seoul Olympics

South Korea hosted the Summer Olympics — showing the world how much the country had rebuilt. The "Korean Miracle" (한강의 기적) was real.

Today

대한민국 · Modern South Korea

South Korea is now a world leader in technology (Samsung, LG, Hyundai), entertainment (K-pop, K-dramas, Parasite), and food. From war-torn rubble in 1953 to global cultural powerhouse in 70 years.
